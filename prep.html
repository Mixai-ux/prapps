Q&A Bank
Likely questions and how to frame your answer.
Walk me through a product you owned end-to-end.
Hard
Use Smart-Reply.AI — discovery, build, and results. Name the LLM stack. Land on FCR +30%.
"At Smart-Reply.AI I owned an LLM-powered customer agent from idea to production. Discovery: 40 user interviews revealed 24/7 coverage as the top pain. I selected OpenAI + RAG with Pinecone. We shipped in 4 months. FCR improved 30%, CSAT +25%."
How do you prioritize when everything feels urgent?
Medium
Name your framework (ICE/RICE/WSJF), give a concrete example from Sephora backlog.
"I use ICE scoring — Impact × Confidence × Ease. At Sephora I had 10 competing features. Two had 9/10 impact but easy = 8. One was gamification (high effort) — I delayed it despite pressure. Checkout optimization shipped first. CR +12% in 2 weeks."
Tell me about a time you failed.
Hard
Use Smart-Reply.AI shutdown — frame as market/investor factors, not personal failure. Show learning.
"Smart-Reply.AI had strong product metrics but we lost our US expansion investor due to sanction risk concerns. I learned: validate funding runway before building for scale, not after product-market fit. I now treat investor alignment as a product constraint from day one."
Why do you want this specific role?
Easy
Connect your AI background to their product challenge. Show you researched them.
"I've spent 3 years building with LLMs — I know where the real complexity lives (not the API call, but the data quality, the edge cases, the feedback loops). [Company] is solving [X] — that's exactly where I want to apply those hard-won lessons."
